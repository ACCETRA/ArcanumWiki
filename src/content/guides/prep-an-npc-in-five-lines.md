Most NPCs do not need a page. They need a shape the DM can remember while people are asking questions at speed.

## The five lines

Write only these:

1. What they want right now.
2. What they are hiding.
3. What they sound like.
4. What they can offer.
5. What makes dealing with them difficult.

That is enough to survive first contact.

## Why this works

The players meet NPCs in motion, not in a wiki dump. A clear want makes the character active. A hidden pressure keeps them from sounding flat. A vocal habit makes them memorable. An offer gives the scene a reason to exist. A difficulty stops them from becoming a vending machine.

## Reuse the same format when an NPC grows

If the apothecary suddenly matters for six sessions, add details under the same headings instead of starting over. That way the page grows from play instead of from your anxiety.

## The warning

Do not confuse lore with readiness. A paragraph about their tragic schooling does less for the next session than a note that says, "owes money to the guard captain and hates being interrupted."
