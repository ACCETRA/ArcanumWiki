A good session zero is not a ceremony. It is maintenance before the campaign has anything expensive to break.

## Start with the campaign promise

Tell the group what kind of game you are offering in one or two sentences. Is this a frontier crawl, a city intrigue game, or a horror campaign where resources stay tight? Players can only make useful characters if they know what the table is trying to become.

## Decide what belongs on the table

Talk through lines, veils, and subject matter that should stay out of the campaign. Keep this part plain. If somebody does not want a topic in the game, that answer does not need a courtroom speech attached to it.

## Lock the practical rules early

Cover the questions that create friction later:

- How often do you meet?
- What happens when someone misses a session?
- Are phones out or away?
- How do you handle house rules and rules disputes mid-session?
- Is PvP off the table?

If you leave these vague, the campaign ends up negotiating them in the worst possible moment.

## Build characters with the actual campaign in mind

Ask each player to answer three things:

- Why is this character willing to work with the group?
- What does this character want in the first ten sessions?
- What part of the world already knows their name?

Those answers create momentum. They also stop the common problem where everyone arrives with a dramatic backstory and no reason to trust anyone else.

## End with a short written recap

Write down the campaign tone, table rules, safety agreements, and any homebrew decisions. If it matters enough to agree on, it matters enough to survive memory.
